About CPUNK Protocol

CPUNK is a post-quantum identity and messaging protocol built on NIST Category 5 cryptography. It combines decentralized DNA identities, secure communication, and community governance into one coherent ecosystem.

NIST Category 5 cryptography (Kyber1024, Dilithium5, SHA3-512) at the core. No centralized ownership – protocol steered by its community.

Our Mission

⬅️ Vision

To create a quantum-safe, decentralized identity protocol that empowers individuals with true ownership and control over their digital identities, while enabling global coordination through secure communication tools.

🔧 Mission

CPUNK is evolving into a comprehensive post-quantum decentralized identity and messaging protocol. Our goal is to provide both community-focused experiences and robust, protocol-level infrastructure that applications and enterprises can build on.

The CPUNK Evolution

From early experiment to full identity & messaging protocol

🧬 Phase 1: Foundation (2025–2026)

  • DNA Registration System: Decentralized Network Applications for unique, human-readable identities.
  • Community Platform: Social features, messaging, voting, and governance on cpunk.club.
  • Validator Delegation: Staking and rewards with a delegation model that supports network security.
  • Multi-language Support: Global accessibility with 8+ supported languages.
  • OAuth Integration: Sign-in via GitHub, Google, and other major platforms where needed.

🛰 Phase 2: Protocol Evolution (2026+)

  • Enhanced DNA System: Digital identity anchored to CF20 wallet public keys, with DHT-backed profiles.
  • Proof of Humanity: A multi-factor verification framework for authentic human users, without sacrificing privacy.
  • Identity Revocation: User-controlled mechanisms for deactivating or rotating identities when needed.
  • Trustless Backend: DHT-first architecture (Nodus network) with local caching instead of centralized databases.
  • Post-Quantum Protocol: End-to-end integration of quantum-safe cryptography for identity, messaging, and key management.

Core Values

Principles that guide how we design and build the protocol

🛡 Security First

Built on NIST Category 5 post-quantum cryptography, CPUNK aims for long-term security against both classical and future quantum threats.

🌐 True Decentralization

Identities and communication should not depend on a single company or server. Ownership is cryptographic, not contractual.

👥 Community Driven

Governance happens through transparent proposals and community decisions. Users, validators, and builders all have a voice.

🔒 Privacy Focused

Your data belongs to you. CPUNK is designed to minimize data collection and expose only what’s needed for the protocol to function.

🚀 Innovation

We experiment with new cryptographic primitives, new UX patterns, and new coordination mechanisms – always with real-world use in mind.

🌍 Accessibility

A global protocol must be understandable and usable by people in many languages and skill levels – not just cryptography experts.

Technology Foundation

🧬 Post-Quantum Foundation

NIST Category 5 Security: CPUNK is built on post-quantum cryptography providing maximum quantum resistance (256-bit security level).

  • Kyber1024 for key encapsulation (ML-KEM-1024)
  • Dilithium5 for digital signatures (ML-DSA-87)
  • AES-256-GCM for symmetric encryption
  • SHA3-512 for cryptographic hashing

⬅️ CPUNK Infrastructure

Dual-Domain Architecture: CPUNK runs two main public surfaces that serve different roles but share the same protocol core.

  • cpunk.club: Community, governance, dashboards, tools
  • cpunk.io: Identity protocol, APIs, and service layer
  • Post-quantum DHT backend (Nodus network) for protocol data
  • REST-style APIs for developers integrating DNA and messaging
