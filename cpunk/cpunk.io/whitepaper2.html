Overview

From community token to protocol layer

CPUNK has evolved from a meme coin into a post-quantum infrastructure layer for decentralized identity and private communication.

DNA Identity

Human-readable names bound to post-quantum public keys. Each DNA identity resolves to a wallet address, messenger endpoint and optional metadata — all on-chain.

DNA-Messenger

Fully decentralized messenger routing E2E encrypted messages through a peer-to-peer DHT network. No servers, no metadata, no phone numbers.

Tokenomics & CIPs

CPUNK is used for DNA registration, delegation and protocol fees. Community Improvement Proposals (CIPs) steer upgrades and treasury allocations.

Security

NIST Category 5 — maximum quantum resistance

256-bit security level. Designed under a zero-trust assumption against pervasive monitoring and "store-now-decrypt-later" attacks.

Algorithm Standard Purpose
Kyber1024 ML-KEM-1024 (FIPS 203) Key encapsulation
Dilithium5 ML-DSA-87 (FIPS 204) Digital signatures
AES-256-GCM NIST Symmetric encryption
SHA3-512 NIST Hashing & integrity
Post-Quantum Keys
Key exchange and signatures resist both classical and future quantum adversaries.
E2E Encryption
All messenger traffic encrypted end-to-end, independent of transport layer.
SHA-3 Integrity
SHA-3 family hashing for commitments, integrity and quantum-resistant identifiers.
Anti-Harvest
Protection against replay, tampering and store-now-decrypt-later attacks.
Governance

Incentives and decentralized decision-making

Tokenomics & CIP Governance

CPUNK coordinates incentives for validators, infrastructure operators and contributors without VC unlocks or a central company. The economic layer and governance work together so the protocol can evolve autonomously.

  • Free DNA registration — no fees to claim your identity
  • CIP-based governance with optional vote delegation
  • Treasury allocations for development and infrastructure
  • Legacy mapping from v1.0 tokenomics to the v2.x protocol model

Proof of Humanity

The whitepaper describes how CPUNK differentiates real humans from bots for social features, without requiring centralized KYC or personal data.

  • Behavior analysis — interaction patterns, not biometrics
  • Web-of-trust — peer attestations between verified identities
  • Reputation scoring — earned through protocol participation
  • Optional attestations — pluggable verification providers
