Built by the Community, for the Community

CPUNK is a open, community-driven protocol for post-quantum identity and messaging. There is no central company and no closed team — anyone with passion for Web3 and the post-quantum future can contribute.

Community Roles & contributions
Protocol:
CPUNK Protocol

CPUNK grows through small, consistent contributions from people across the world. You do not need to be a full-time developer to have impact.

The project depends on a mix of code, infrastructure, writing, design, research, and community work.

Develop: DNA-Messenger, tooling, libraries, integrations.
Operate: Validators and infrastructure that secure the network.
Explain: Documentation, articles, tutorials, translations.
Coordinate: Governance, proposals, and community organization.

Everyone can contribute

Your skills, your impact

CPUNK isn't owned by a company or controlled by a small group. It’s a protocol that moves forward when the community does.

Code Development

Build the protocol & tools

Contribute to open-source repositories, implement new features, improve performance, harden security, and help maintain the DNA-Messenger and supporting services.

Design UX & Brand

Design the experience

Work on UI/UX for apps and websites, create visuals, animations, infographics, and branding elements that explain CPUNK to new users.

Write Docs & Content

Explain and document

Write documentation, tutorials, blog posts, and translations. Help others understand how CPUNK, DNA, and post-quantum cryptography fit together.

Validate Nodes

Run validators & nodes

Operate nodes and validators that process transactions, validate DNA registrations, and participate in consensus to secure the post-quantum network.

Advocate Community

Grow the community

Share updates on social media, invite new contributors, organize calls or local meetups, and help connect CPUNK with the broader post-quantum and Web3 ecosystem.

Govern Direction

Shape the roadmap

Participate in discussions, proposals, and community decisions. Help align development priorities with long-term protocol goals.

Validator Network

Decentralized infrastructure securing CPUNK

A distributed set of node operators and validators maintain the CPUNK protocol, validate DNA registrations, and keep the network reliable.

Node operators & validators

The validator network plays a critical role in securing the CPUNK protocol and its identity and messaging layers.

  • Validate DNA registrations and identity bindings
  • Process and verify transactions on the network
  • Participate in consensus and protocol upgrades
  • Help maintain global uptime and reliability
  • Support the post-quantum security model of CPUNK
